# Reel 03 — Dead Sea Mini-Reel

**Account:** Jordan Unveiled · Travel & Tourism
**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · 30 fps · 15s · 4 clips
**Audience:** Wellness & relaxation travellers 25–45, Gulf + Europe
**Tone:** Calm, premium, slightly playful
**Goal:** Stop the scroll, build desire, maximize saves + shares

---

## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Open on the float-and-read shot — a person lying back on the water surface,
relaxed, reading a newspaper, feet up.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **You can't sink here.** | Playful, slightly disbelief-triggering, pairs perfectly with the float visual. |
| B | The lowest point on Earth. | Strong fact, but cooler/educational — slower emotional pull. |
| C | Float, don't swim. | Punchy and clever, but a touch cryptic on its own. |

**Recommended: A — "You can't sink here."**
It states something the brain wants to argue with, while the visual instantly
proves it. That tension (claim + proof on screen) is the scroll-stopper, and it
fits the calm/playful tone better than the cooler "lowest point" stat.

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Pairing:** clean modern — headline in a geometric sans (Montserrat
  SemiBold / Poppins), support in the same family Light.
- **Animation:** soft fade-up reveals; lines ease in, no hard snaps — matches
  the calm tone.
- Max **4 words per line.**
- Light type with a subtle shadow over water; keep it airy, lots of negative
  space.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** soft turquoise water, mineral white salt, warm sunset gold.
- Lift blacks slightly for a soft, premium-spa feel (low contrast, not flat).
- Keep skin tones natural and warm; whites clean and bright.
- Sunset clip warms fully into gold; everything else stays cool-calm — one
  cohesive low-contrast grade.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- Relaxed **but rhythmic** — cuts land on the beat, but moves are slow and
  gentle (no fast whips). Calm ≠ sloppy.
- Gentle easing on every move; the macro mud push-in (Clip 2) is the most
  "active" moment.
- Let the sunset clip breathe longest into the CTA.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** CapCut for the gentle beat-sync and fade-ups; the built-in
smooth-slow and dissolve presets fit the calm grade well.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **float-and-read opening
selling "you can't sink" in the first second.** The visual *is* the proof of
the hook — if the float looks effortless and a little funny (newspaper, feet
up), the reel lands. A weak/staged float kills it.

**Footage to source:** the float-and-read needs a real shot (with a dry prop
newspaper); mud ritual macro; a wide establishing shot (drone or high shore
vantage); and a clean sunset clip. Flag: the float shot is the make-or-break —
plan it deliberately rather than grabbing it.
