# Reel 08 — Arabic Travel Phrases Reel (Animated Phrase Cards)

**Account:** Jordan Unveiled · Travel & Tourism
**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · 30 fps · 20s
**Audience:** First-time visitors to Jordan 22–45, Europe + worldwide
**Tone:** Friendly, helpful, polished
**Goal:** Deliver instant practical value → saves + shares

> **Bilingual display rule (critical):** Arabic and English never share a line.
> Each card stacks three separate lines, top to bottom:
> **1) Arabic (own line) → 2) English (own line) → 3) Pronunciation (own line).**
> Never embed English mid-Arabic.

---

## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Clean branded title card, soft gradient or light Jordan b-roll behind.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **Say this in Jordan.** | Short, direct, implies instant utility. Reads in under a second. |
| B | 5 Arabic phrases that help instantly. | Clear value + number, but longer to read. |
| C | Locals will love you for these. | Warm emotional payoff, but vaguer on what you get. |

**Recommended: A — "Say this in Jordan."**
Shortest and most action-oriented — it reads instantly on a text-heavy reel
where the cards already carry the detail. It frames the whole reel as "do this"
practical content, which is exactly what gets saved. Pair it with a small
"5 phrases" sub-line so the count is still visible.

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Arabic:** large, clear Arabic type (e.g. Cairo, Tajawal, or Noto Naskh
  Arabic) — biggest element on the card, top line.
- **English:** clean Latin sans (Montserrat Medium) — second line, clearly
  smaller than the Arabic.
- **Pronunciation:** same Latin family, Italic / lighter weight — third line.
- **Sequence per card:** Arabic line animates in first, then English fades up
  below, then pronunciation — so the eye learns top-to-bottom.
- One line per element; generous line spacing; never overlap Arabic and Latin.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** clean modern brand palette (warm sand + deep teal/charcoal),
  high legibility.
- Background: subtle light gradient **or** very soft, blurred Jordan b-roll
  (Petra/Wadi Rum) at low opacity behind the cards, gently drifting.
- Strong contrast between text and background — dark text on light, or white
  text on a darkened scrim. Keep it calm and uncluttered.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- Steady, even card rhythm — equal ~3s per phrase so nothing feels rushed.
- Smooth slide/fade transitions between phrases (consistent direction).
- Subtle continuous background motion (slow gradient drift or b-roll pan) so the
  frame never feels static, without distracting from reading.

Light, playful, low-energy track that supports reading without competing.

Track-style references:

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** After Effects or Canva (Pro) for clean, controllable text
animation and reliable **right-to-left Arabic rendering**; CapCut works but
test Arabic shaping carefully (many editors break Arabic letter-joining).

**The single most important thing to nail:** **correct, properly-shaped Arabic
text with the strict 3-line stacking and ≥2.5s readability.** If the Arabic
renders disconnected/backwards, or a line is too small/too fast to read, the
value evaporates and the reel won't be saved. Verify Arabic shaping on the
rendered export, not just in the editor preview.

**Footage to source:** minimal — optional soft Jordan b-roll for backgrounds
(reuse from other reels). Flag: have a native Jordanian Arabic speaker verify
spelling, dialect, and pronunciation transliteration before publishing.
