# Reel 07 — Food Reel: Amman Street Food Crawl

**Account:** Jordan Unveiled · Travel & Tourism
**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · 30 fps · 20s · 5 clips
**Audience:** Budget & food-experience travellers 22–40, Gulf + Europe
**Tone:** Vibrant, energetic, mouth-watering
**Goal:** Stop the scroll, trigger appetite, maximize saves + shares

---

## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Open on the sizzling falafel drop — balls hitting hot oil, bubbles and sizzle in
macro.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **$5 street food in Jordan.** | Value hook — price + place. Save-worthy for budget travellers, instantly clear. |
| B | Falafel like you've never had. | Bold but unprovable claim; weaker for cold viewers. |
| C | Eat your way through Amman. | Good crawl framing, but soft as an opener. |

**Recommended: A — "$5 street food in Jordan."**
The price tag is the scroll-stopper and the save-trigger for this budget-minded
audience — it promises concrete value the sizzling visual makes irresistible.
"$5" also reads instantly and travels well across Gulf + Europe viewers. Use C
as the CTA-adjacent line.

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Captions:** punchy bold sans (Montserrat ExtraBold / Anton), uppercase for
  the hook.
- **Animation:** quick **pop-in** — text scales up fast and settles on the beat;
  snappy, energetic.
- Max **4 words per line.**
- High-contrast white type with a bold shadow or a small colored highlight box
  behind key words ("$5"); keep off the food, anchored top safe zone.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** vibrant, warm, juicy; high appetite appeal.
- Push saturation and warmth; deepen the fry golds, brighten the hummus, make
  the olive oil glisten.
- Higher contrast than the Mansaf reel — street food = punchy and alive.
- Consistent vibrant grade across all five clips.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- Fast, punchy, **beat-synced to each sizzle and dip** — quick cuts, lots of
  energy.
- Snap zooms and flash cuts on the beat; no slow drifts here.
- Build momentum toward the final bite-grab.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** CapCut — fast beat-sync, snap-zoom presets, and easy foley
layering make it ideal for a punchy 5-clip street-food cutdown.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **sizzling falafel drop in the
first second.** Hot oil + sizzle is one of the most reliable scroll-stoppers in
food content — shoot it macro at 60–120fps with good side light so the bubbles
and steam pop. Nail that and the energy carries.

**Footage to source:** all macro/process street-food footage shot fresh —
falafel fry, hummus swirl + pour, shawarma carve, bread pull. Flag: capture
clean **foley audio on location** (sizzle, knife, crunch) — it's as important as
the visuals and hard to fake convincingly later.
