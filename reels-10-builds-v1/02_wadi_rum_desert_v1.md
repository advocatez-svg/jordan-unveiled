# Reel 02 — Wadi Rum Desert Mini-Reel

**Account:** Jordan Unveiled · Travel & Tourism
**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · 30 fps · 15s · 4 clips
**Audience:** Adventure & nature travellers 25–45, Gulf + Europe
**Tone:** Epic, awe-inspiring, premium
**Goal:** Stop the scroll, build desire, maximize saves + shares

---

## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Open on a sweeping aerial over the red dunes at golden hour — long shadows,
endless sand, a 4×4 as a tiny dot for scale.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **Mars on Earth.** | Instant visual hook, 3 words, universally understood, screenshot-able. |
| B | This is Wadi Rum. | Clear but flat — assumes the name carries weight. |
| C | Sleep under a billion stars. | Beautiful, but pays off the night clip, not the opening dune. |

**Recommended: A — "Mars on Earth."**
Three words, zero context required, and it perfectly matches the red-dune
aerial on screen. It's a known meme/association that makes cold viewers stop
and think "where *is* that?" — then the reveal answers "Wadi Rum, Jordan."

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Headline:** bold condensed (Anton / Bebas Neue), uppercase.
- **Support:** light weight (Montserrat Light / Helvetica Now Light).
- **Animation:** slide-up + fade synced to the beat; headline holds, support
  fades up beneath it.
- Max **4–5 words per line.**
- High-contrast white type with a subtle shadow; on the night clip switch to
  pure white at full opacity over the dark sky.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** deep rust red, saturated orange dunes, cobalt night sky.
- Push warmth and red saturation in dune clips; protect highlight detail on
  sunlit sand (don't clip).
- Night clip: cool the shadows toward cobalt, keep stars crisp, warm the
  firelight for contrast.
- One consistent grade spine; let the night clip be the deliberate cool
  counterpoint to three warm clips.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- Epic, wide, breathing pacing on Clips 1–2; tighten on Clip 3.
- **Signature move:** one **speed-ramp on the jeep drive (Clip 3)** — ramp from
  slow-mo into real-time (or real-time into fast) right as the jeep crosses
  frame, landing on the beat drop.
- Ease all drone moves; no jerky gimbal corrections.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** CapCut for the speed-ramp + beat-sync (its built-in curve speed
tool is fast); DaVinci Resolve if you want cleaner optical-flow retiming on the
jeep ramp.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **speed-ramp hitting the beat
drop at ~7.5s.** The whole reel builds to that one kinetic moment — ramp timing
and drop must share a frame.

**Footage to source:** drone aerial of dunes at golden hour (Clip 1), rock
formation low-angle (Clip 2), a **planned jeep-drive pass shot at higher frame
rate** (60–120fps) so you can ramp it (Clip 3), and a long-exposure-friendly
night/astro clip of the camp (Clip 4). Flag: night Milky Way footage is the
hardest to get clean — if unavailable, source a licensed astro timelapse and
match the grade.
