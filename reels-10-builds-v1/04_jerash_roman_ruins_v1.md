## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Headline:** bold condensed (Anton / Bebas Neue), uppercase.
- **Support:** light weight (Montserrat Light), italic for location tags.
- **Animation:** vertical **mask reveals** — lines wipe up to match the upward
  architecture; synced to the beat.
- Max **4 words per line.**
- Warm-white type with shadow; scrim behind text on bright sky frames.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** warm honey stone, deep blue sky, golden-hour finish.
- Push warmth into the limestone; keep the sky a clean saturated blue for the
  honey/blue contrast that makes ruins pop.
- Final clip warms fully to golden hour.
- One grade across all four so arch, plaza, street and theatre feel unified.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- Smooth, stately push-ins and pans — grandeur, not speed.
- **Signature move:** one **parallax move along the colonnade (Clip 2)** —
  foreground columns sliding faster than the background ring for depth.
- Ease everything; one swell-timed beat carries the energy.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** After Effects for a true parallax move on the colonnade (or
CapCut's keyframe zoom/position for a lighter 2.5D version); edit + grade in
Premiere/CapCut.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **"This isn't Italy / It's
Jordan" misdirection in the first second.** The entire hook is the double-take —
the opening frame must look convincingly Roman/European so the "It's Jordan"
reveal pays off. Pick your most classically-Roman-looking colonnade frame to open.

**Footage to source:** Hadrian's Arch hero shot, Oval Plaza colonnade (shoot
with foreground depth for the parallax), Cardo street push, and South Theatre.
All real Jerash footage. Flag: golden-hour timing matters — the honey-stone
grade depends on warm light, so shoot late afternoon.
