# Reel 09 — Photo-to-Video Cinematic Reel

**Account:** Jordan Unveiled · Travel & Tourism
**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · 30 fps · 20s · ~5 stills
**Audience:** Travellers 25–45, Gulf + Europe deciding where to go next
**Tone:** Emotional, aspirational, premium
**Goal:** Turn stills into a premium cinematic montage that drives saves + shares
**Use case:** Weeks with limited video footage

---

## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Open on the most dramatic still — the Petra Treasury — with a slow push-in so
the static image already feels like film.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **Jordan in 20 seconds.** | Promises a complete, fast payoff — sets a clear watch-to-end expectation. |
| B | Proof you need to go. | Punchy, persuasive, but vaguer. |
| C | Save this before you book. | Action-led, but pre-empts the CTA too early. |

**Recommended: A — "Jordan in 20 seconds."**
It sets a precise, finishable promise ("20 seconds") that maximises watch-through
on a montage — viewers stay to collect the whole country. It also frames the reel
as a tidy, save-worthy summary, which is exactly the behaviour this format wants.

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Headline:** bold condensed (Anton / Bebas Neue), uppercase.
- **Support:** light sans (Montserrat Light), italic for location tags.
- **Animation:** soft **fade-up** reveals; one location tag per still, timed to
  the cut.
- Max **3–4 words per line.**
- Warm-white type with a subtle shadow; keep within the safe zone over busy
  stills.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **One consistent cinematic grade applied to all stills** so the five photos
  feel like a single film, not a gallery.
- Direction: warm, filmic, gentle teal-in-shadow / warm-highlight; lifted blacks
  for a soft premium feel.
- Match exposure and white balance across stills first, *then* apply the unifying
  LUT — mismatched source photos are the #1 thing that breaks the "one film"
  illusion.

---

## 5. Motion & Pacing

- **Continuous gentle motion on every still — no static frames.** Every photo is
  always moving (zoom, drift, or parallax).
- Smooth crossfades between most stills; **optional light leaks** over 1–2
  transitions for warmth.
- **One speed-ramp transition** into the Dead Sea (Photo 3) for a single kinetic
  accent, landing on the music's rise.
- Keep motion slow and eased — premium, not frantic.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** **CapCut** for fast Ken Burns + crossfades + light leaks across
five stills; use **After Effects** (or the **Photoshop → AE** depth workflow,
or apps like **CapCut's AI motion / LeiaPix / Luma**) for the **2.5D parallax**
on the Treasury and Dead Sea stills.

**2.5D parallax workflow (per hero still):**
1. Open the still; separate foreground / midground / background onto layers
   (mask + content-aware fill the gaps), **or** use a depth-map / AI 2.5D tool.
2. Offset the layers in Z (or apply a depth displacement).
3. Animate a slow camera push so foreground moves faster than background → real
   parallax depth from a flat photo.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **one unified grade so five
separate photos read as a single film.** Photo-to-video lives or dies on
cohesion — match exposure/WB and apply one LUT across all stills. If they look
like five different photos, the premium illusion is gone.

**Footage to source:** none new — this is the limited-footage format. Flag:
**check resolution on every still — each must be ≥1080px on the short edge** (ideally
much larger so push-ins don't soften). Upscale low-res stills before motion, and
flag any photo that can't hold a zoom without going soft.
