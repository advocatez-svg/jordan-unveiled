## 1. Hook (1–2s)

Open on a tight slow-motion jameed pour — warm, creamy sauce streaming over
rice and lamb, steam rising.

| # | Hook line | Read |
|---|-----------|------|
| A | **Jordan's national dish.** | Clear stakes + authority; tells you why this matters in 3 words. |
| B | You eat this with your hands. | Intriguing/interactive, but a process detail, not the headline. |
| C | The most Jordanian thing you can taste. | Evocative but vague and longer. |

**Recommended: A — "Jordan's national dish."**
For a food reel the appetite is carried by the *visual* (the pour), so the text
should add stakes, not compete. "National dish" instantly frames Mansaf as the
must-try, authoritative, save-worthy centerpiece — the strongest reason to
bookmark. Save B as an on-screen line for a later clip.

## 3. Kinetic Typography

- **Headline:** clean bold sans (Montserrat Bold / Anton for the hook line).
- **Captions:** fade-up element labels naming each component (jameed, lamb,
  shrak bread).
- **Animation:** soft fade-up; labels appear as each element hits frame, synced
  to the beat.
- Max **4 words per line.**
- Warm-white type with a subtle shadow; keep text off the food itself — anchor
  to the top safe zone so the dish stays hero.

---

## 4. Color & Grade

- **Palette:** warm, rich, appetizing; lifted highlights on the food.
- Push warmth and gentle saturation into the lamb and sauce; keep rice bright
  and clean (no yellow-green cast).
- Lift highlights so steam and glisten read; protect the white jameed from
  blowing out.
- Slightly creamy, premium-restaurant grade across all five clips.

## How to Build This

**Best tool:** CapCut for the ASMR beat-sync and slow-mo; layer foley on its
audio tracks. Shoot macro on a phone with a clip-on macro lens or a mirrorless
with a 100mm macro.

**The single most important thing to nail:** the **slow-motion jameed pour in
the first second.** The pour *is* the appetite trigger and the scroll-stopper —
shoot it at 120fps, get clean steam and a creamy stream, and the whole reel
works. A flat, fast pour loses the room.

**Footage to source:** all macro food footage must be shot fresh — pour, lamb,
bread tear, scoop, and the full-plate reveal. Plate it generously and shoot the
pour multiple times. Flag: steam reads best with a dark, contrasty background
and side light — style the set before rolling.
